The **Espace Zoologique de Saint-Martin-la-Plaine** is literally at the end of the street —
a few hundred metres from the apartment. It is one of the most committed wildlife parks
in France for the **conservation of endangered species**, with 600 animals spread across
26 hectares.

<!--more-->

## Animals not to be missed

The park is internationally recognised for its **western lowland gorilla conservation
programme**, one of the most significant captive populations in Europe. They can be
observed from spacious enclosures designed for their well-being.

Among the other residents:

- **Giraffes, zebras, rhinoceroses** in large African savannah spaces
- **Asian elephants** — family group with young elephants
- **Big cats**: lions, cheetahs, clouded leopards
- **Primates**: bonobos, mandrills, orang-utans
- **Birds**: flamingos, marabou storks, crowned cranes
- **Reptiles** and amphibians in a dedicated space

## Commitment to conservation

The Espace Zoologique actively participates in **European breeding programmes (EEP)**
for several critically endangered species. Part of the admission fee directly funds
conservation projects in the wild.

## In practice with children

The park offers **guided tours**, activities at weekends and during school holidays,
as well as a picnic area. Allow 3 to 4 hours to make the most of the visit.
Pushchairs and wheelchairs can access the majority of the paths.
