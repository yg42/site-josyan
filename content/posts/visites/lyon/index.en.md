Lyon, **40 minutes by TER** from Rive-de-Gier, is one of the most beautiful cities in France.
Gastronomy, UNESCO heritage, world-class museums: a single day is not enough.

<!--more-->

## Vieux-Lyon and Fourvière

**Vieux-Lyon**, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest Renaissance ensemble
in Europe after the historic centre of Florence. Its *traboules* (secret passageways
between buildings) form a fascinating labyrinth to explore.

On the hill above, the **Basilique Notre-Dame de Fourvière** offers an exceptional panorama
over the city and, on a clear day, over the Alps.

## Gastronomy

Lyon is the world capital of gastronomy. Not to be missed:
- The **bouchons lyonnais**: traditional cuisine (quenelles, andouillette, tablier de sapeur)
- The **markets**: Marché de la Croix-Rousse (Tuesday–Sunday morning), Halles Paul Bocuse
- The **Halles Paul Bocuse**: temple of Lyon's finest produce

## Museums

- **Musée des Confluences** — sciences, anthropology, natural history (already featured on this site)
- **Musée des Beaux-Arts** — among the richest in France
- **Musée Gadagne** — history of Lyon and puppet museum (Guignol!)
- **Institut Lumière** — birthplace of cinema, in Monplaisir

## Getting around Lyon

From Lyon Part-Dieu, the TCL network (metro + tram) covers the entire city.
Bike-sharing: **Vélo'v** available everywhere.

## Practical info

- 🚂 **TER from Rive-de-Gier**: ~40 min to Lyon Part-Dieu
