**45 minutes** from the apartment, the **Musée des Confluences** is one of the most
spectacular museums in France. Its crystal-like building, seemingly floating at the tip
of the Lyon peninsula, is as much an architectural statement as an invitation to curiosity.

<!--more-->

## The building

Designed by the Austrian firm **Coop Himmelblau**, the building opened in 2014 and is
an architectural feat: a steel and glass structure suspended above the confluence of the
Rhône and Saône rivers. The visit begins before you even step inside.

## The permanent collections

The museum is organised around four major galleries:

- **Origins** — the universe, the Earth, life, and the human being
- **Societies** — human organisation across cultures and ages
- **Eternities** — beliefs, death, and the sacred in all civilisations
- **Confluences** — the interconnectedness of the contemporary world

The collections blend minerals, fossils, Egyptian mummies, primitive art objects,
dinosaur skeletons and digital installations. Ideal for all ages.

## Temporary exhibitions

Temporary exhibitions are ambitious and regularly renewed. Check the programme
before your visit — some are exceptional.

## With the family

The museum is particularly family-friendly: kids' spaces, audio guides, workshops
during school holidays. Allow **2 to 3 hours** minimum for a complete visit.

## Practical info

- 📍 **Musée des Confluences** — 86 quai Perrache, Lyon 2e
- 🚗 45 min by car / accessible by TER + tram T1 from Lyon
- 💶 Adult: ~€12 — free for under 18s
