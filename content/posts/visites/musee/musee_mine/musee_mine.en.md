**20 minutes** from Saint-Martin-la-Plaine, the **Musée de la Mine de Saint-Étienne** takes
visitors down into the reconstructed galleries of a coal mine. An immersive and moving
experience that brings to life the daily reality of the miners of Saint-Étienne.

<!--more-->

## A descent into the depths of the earth

The museum is built around the **puits Couriot**, a former extraction shaft of the Loire
coalfield. Guided tours take groups down into galleries reconstructed to the last detail:
the noise of machinery, confined atmosphere, period tools.

You will discover:

- Working conditions underground, from the late 19th century through to the 1970s
- Extraction machinery and pit ponies
- Life at the pithead — locker rooms, lamp room, hanging room
- Audio and visual testimonies from miners and their families

## A place of memory

Saint-Étienne was for two centuries the **coal capital of France**. The museum
pays tribute to generations of miners, often immigrants, whose work forged
the identity of the entire region.

## In practice

The visit is **guided and compulsory**, with departures every hour. Duration: approximately
1h30. Closed-toe shoes are required. The visit is suitable from **6–7 years old**.
