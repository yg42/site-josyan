**20 minutes** from the apartment, the **Musée d'Art et d'Industrie de Saint-Étienne**
is one of the most original museums in the region. It explores the industrial identity
of Saint-Étienne through three emblematic collections: **ribbon**, **bicycle**,
and **weapons**.

<!--more-->

## Three unique collections in the world

### Ribbon

Saint-Étienne was for two centuries the world capital of passementerie (decorative trimming).
The museum holds an extraordinary collection of ribbons, braids, lace and
industrial embroidery — objects that are at once technical and artistic, witnesses
to a craft now largely lost.

### Bicycle

The city played a pioneering role in bicycle manufacturing in France from the late
19th century onwards. The collection traces the history of the bicycle from the earliest
velocipedes to racing bikes, with rare and spectacular pieces.

### Weapons

A royal arms factory since the 17th century, Saint-Étienne supplied the French armies
for centuries. The collection presents rifles, pistols and prestige weapons,
with a focus on the national manufacture still in operation today.

## The building

The museum is housed in the **former Palais des Arts**, a neo-classical
19th-century building on Place Louis-Comte. The architecture itself is worth a look.
