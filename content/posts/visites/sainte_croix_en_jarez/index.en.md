Just **15 minutes** from Saint-Martin-la-Plaine, Sainte-Croix-en-Jarez is one of the most
singular villages in the region: the entire village is built inside a former
**medieval charterhouse** from the 13th century.

<!--more-->

## An inhabited charterhouse

The Chartreuse de Sainte-Croix-en-Jarez was founded in 1280. After the Revolution, when the
monks left, peasant families moved in and still live there today.
The result is unique: a village community occupying the original cloisters, cells and
conventual buildings.

You can wander freely through the inner lanes, past flower-filled houses, fountains
and a restored chapel. A place outside of time.

## La fromagerie des Chartreux

Just a short walk from the village, the [Fromagerie des Chartreux](/posts/visites/chartreux/) offers
artisan cheeses and fresh dairy products. Ideal for combining a cultural visit with a
gourmet stop.

## Hikes around the village

The village is surrounded by trails winding through the hills of the Jarez.
Several marked circuits start directly from the ramparts.
