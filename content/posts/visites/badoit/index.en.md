Just **25 minutes** from Saint-Martin-la-Plaine, Saint-Galmier is the town that sparkles —
birthplace of the famous sparkling water **Badoit**, whose spring has been in use since 1837.

<!--more-->

## A bit of history

The spring was already known in Roman antiquity under the name **Fontfort** (sparkling fountain).
In 1837, **Auguste Saturnin Badoit** secured the lease of the spring and industrialised it,
creating a distribution network across France. The factory, now owned by **Danone**,
is listed as a **Historic Monument**.

## What to see

### Guided factory tour ★
The flagship visit: a 2-hour immersion behind the scenes of production, from water capture to bottling.

- 🕐 Monday to Friday (advance booking required)
- 👶 From age 8
- 💶 7 €/adult — 5 €/child (8–17 years)
- 👟 Dress code: closed-toe shoes, trousers, long sleeves
- 📞 Booking: 06 62 93 49 43 or [saintetiennetourisme.tickeasy.com](https://saintetiennetourisme.tickeasy.com/fr-FR/accueil)

### Espace Découverte Badoit — free admission
A museum area with free access:
- Brand history in video and display panels
- 19th-century bottling wells
- Merchandise shop

### Tapping kiosk
In a park at the heart of the town, a kiosk lets you **drink and draw the naturally sparkling
Badoit water free of charge** — a unique experience.

- 🗓️ Open 1 April to 31 October: Mon–Fri 7:30am–6:30pm / Sat 7:30am–12pm

### Old town and rose garden
- Stroll through the old town (guided tours available)
- **Rose garden** with over 350 varieties
- Stop on the **Chemin de Saint-Jacques-de-Compostelle**
