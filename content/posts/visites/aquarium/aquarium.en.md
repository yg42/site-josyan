The **Lyon Aquarium** is the largest freshwater aquarium in Europe, just **45 minutes**
from Saint-Martin-la-Plaine. Across 1,500 m², it is home to more than **300 species** from
rivers, streams and seas around the world — a fascinating journey for young and old alike.

<!--more-->

## The trail

The trail is organised around major aquatic ecosystems:

- **Rivers of the world** — Amazon, Congo, Mekong, Rhône… with piranhas, arowanas and electric eels
- **Mediterranean and marine tanks** — rays, moray eels, groupers
- **The shark tank** — viewable across several levels, the highlight of the visit
- **The mangrove** and tropical zones
- **Rare specimens**: ocean sunfish, reconstructed coelacanth, abyssal species

## Perfect for families

The aquarium offers **regular activities**: commented shark feeding,
children's workshops during school holidays. Allow **1h30 to 2h** for the visit.
The museum is entirely indoors — perfect for bad weather.

## Getting there by public transport from Saint-Martin-la-Plaine

From Rive-de-Gier TER station, take the train to Lyon Part-Dieu then metro line D towards
Gare de Vaise. The aquarium is nearby.
